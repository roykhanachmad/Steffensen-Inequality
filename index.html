Steffensen's Inequality Visualizer

Discrete Version and Its Generalizations

Theorem 1 (Steffensen's Inequality on Measure Space)
Let $f, g \in L^{1}(X)$ such that $0 \le g \le 1$ a.e. on $X$. Let $U, V \in \mathcal{A}$ be upper-separating and lower-separating sets for $f$, respectively, such that $\mu(U) = \mu(V) = \int_X g \, d\mu$. Then
$$\int_V f \, d\mu \le \int_X fg \, d\mu \le \int_U f \, d\mu.$$
Theorem 2 (Classical Discrete Steffensen)
Let $(x_{i})_{i=1}^{n}$ be a nonincreasing finite sequence of nonnegative real numbers, and let $(y_{i})_{i=1}^{n}$ be a finite sequence such that $0\le y_{i}\le 1$ for all $i\in\{1,...,n\}$. Let $k_{1},k_{2}\in\{1,...,n\}$ such that $k_{2}\le\sum_{i=1}^{n}y_{i}\le k_{1}$, then
$$\sum_{i=n-k_{2}+1}^{n}x_{i}\le\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_{i}y_{i}\le\sum_{i=1}^{k_{1}}x_{i}.$$
Theorem 3 (Measure Space Lower Bound)
Let $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ be a continuous measure space with $\mu(X)<\infty$. Let $0\le\alpha\le 1$ and $f, g$ be functions on $X$ such that $f, f^{\alpha}g\in L^{1}(X)$ and $f,g\ge 0$ a.e. on $X$. Define $\lambda=(\int_{X}g~d\mu)^{\alpha}$ with $\int_{X}g~d\mu>0$. Let $V$ be a lower-separating set for $f$ such that $\mu(V)=\lambda$. Assume that $g\cdot(\int_{X}g~d\mu)^{\alpha-1}\le 1$ a.e. on $V$, then
$$\int_{V}f^{\alpha}d\mu\le\left(\int_{X}gf~d\mu\right)^{\alpha}.$$
Theorem 4 (Discrete Lower Bound)
Let $0\le\alpha\le 1$ and $(x_{i})_{i=1}^{n}$ be a nonincreasing finite sequence of nonnegative real numbers, and let $(y_{i})_{i=1}^{n}$ be a finite sequence of nonnegative real numbers. Let $k\in\mathbb{N}_{n}$ such that $k\le(\sum_{i=1}^{n}y_{i})^{\alpha}$. Assume that $y_{i}(\sum_{j=1}^{n}y_{j})^{\alpha-1}\le 1$ for every $i\in I_{k}$, then
$$\sum_{i=n-k+1}^{n}x_{i}^{\alpha}\le\left(\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_{i}y_{i}\right)^{\alpha}.$$
Theorem 5 (Generalized Discrete Lower Bound)
Let $0<\beta\le 1-\alpha$ with $0\le\alpha<1$, and let $(x_{i})_{i=1}^{n}$ be a nonincreasing finite sequence of nonnegative real numbers, and $(y_{i})_{i=1}^{n}$ be a finite sequence of nonnegative real numbers. Let $k\in\mathbb{N}_{n}$ such that $k\le(\sum_{i=1}^{n}y_{i})^{\frac{\beta}{1-\alpha}}$. Assume that $y_{i}(\sum_{j=1}^{n}y_{j})^{\frac{\beta+\alpha-1}{1-\alpha}}\le 1$ for every $i\in I_{k}$, then
$$\sum_{i=n-k+1}^{n}x_{i}^{\beta}\le\left(\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_{i}^{1-\alpha}y_{i}\right)^{\frac{\beta}{1-\alpha}}.$$
Theorem 6 (Application of Discrete Lower Bound)
Let $0<\beta\le 1-\alpha$ with $0\le\alpha<1$ and $(x_{i})_{i=1}^{n}$ be a nonincreasing finite sequence of nonnegative real numbers. Assume that $\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_{i}\le A$ and $\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_{i}^{\alpha}\ge B^{\alpha}$ where $A, B\in\mathbb{R}^{+}$. Let $k\in\mathbb{N}_{n}$ such that $k\le\left(\frac{B}{A}\right)^{\frac{\alpha\beta}{(\alpha+\beta)(1-\alpha)}}$. If $x_{i}\le\left(\frac{A^{\beta}}{B^{\alpha(\beta+\alpha-1)}}\right)^{\frac{1}{(\alpha+\beta)(1-\alpha)}}$ for every $i\in\mathbb{N}_{n}$, then
$$\sum_{i=n-k+1}^{n}x_{i}^{\beta}\le\left(\frac{A^{\beta}}{B^{\alpha(\beta+\alpha-1)}}\right)^{\frac{\beta}{(\alpha+\beta)(1-\alpha)}}.$$
